Act IV - Episode 1

Why I Stopped Trusting Rented Infrastructure

Tools becoming dramatically more expensive...

Like most people do. A tool for everything. A platform for everything. A subscription for everything.
Jump to content

Intro

At first...

I loved software.

Like most people do.

It felt magical.

A tool for everything.

A platform for everything.

A subscription for everything.

THE DEFAULT ANSWER

Need storage?

Buy software.

Need communication?

Buy software.

Need project management?

Buy software.

Need design?

Buy software.

Need analytics?

Buy software.

Need AI?

Buy software.

At first...

that feels efficient.

Core realization

Until you zoom out.

And realize...

your entire life is running...

on rented infrastructure.

THE PATTERN FELT FAMILIAR

That realization hit me hard.

Because I had already spent years...

watching fragile systems fail.

Corporate systems.

Employment systems.

Operational systems.

And now I was watching...

the same thing happen digitally.

DIGITAL FRAGILITY

Pricing changes.

Feature removals.

Platform shutdowns.

Terms of service changes.

API restrictions.

Vendor lock-in.

Subscription stacking.

Tools becoming dramatically more expensive...

once dependency is established.

Core realization

That pattern felt very familiar.

And very dangerous.

THE BIG QUESTION

Because if your business relies entirely...

on tools you do not control...

you do not fully own your business.

That thought stayed with me.

And it kept getting louder.

THE STACK KEPT GROWING

Especially as I built more projects.

More apps.

More products.

More workflows.

More media systems.

More operational infrastructure.

And the dependency stack kept growing.

Core realization

Eventually...

I asked a very uncomfortable question.

How much of my future...

am I renting?

THE HONEST ANSWER

And the honest answer was...

too much.

Way too much.

EVERYTHING CHANGED

And once I saw that clearly...

I couldn’t pretend it was normal anymore.

I started seeing subscriptions differently.

Some were worth it.

Many were convenience traps.

REWRITING THE RULES

I started asking better questions.

What should be owned?

What should be rented?

What should be local?

What should be cloud-based?

What creates dependency?

What creates resilience?

Core realization

Those questions changed everything.

And they completely reshaped...

how I think about architecture.

THE WEIRD DECISION

And eventually...

those questions pushed me toward something...

that looked ridiculous to some people.

Building my own infrastructure.

At home.

On systems I controlled.

Core realization

That decision changed everything.

Because once I started building owned infrastructure...

my house stopped feeling like just a house.

And started feeling like the early version...

of something much bigger.

Closing

That is where AeroVista begins.

And that story gets very interesting.

Because I got tired...

of renting my future.

So I started building one...

I could actually control.
